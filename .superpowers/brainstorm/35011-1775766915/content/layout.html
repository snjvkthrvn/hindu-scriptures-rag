How should history and navigation live alongside the chat?

The current app is a centered 860px column — one sacred manuscript. Adding history is a layout decision, and the choice sets the tone: meditative single-column vs. tool-like workspace. These are three ways to hold both.

A · Overlay Drawer

Hidden by default, slides in when summoned. A small ☰ button in the header opens a slide-over drawer from the left. The 860px centered column stays exactly as it is — untouched, meditative. On mobile, identical behavior. History is available but invisible until asked for.

Feels like: a single sacred manuscript with a bookmark ribbon tucked behind the cover.

B · Collapsible Rail

Always-visible thin rail, expands on demand. A 40-60px rail on the left holds icons: Om (home), new chat, history toggle, theme toggle. Click the ☰ icon to expand to a full 260px sidebar with thread list. Main content stays at ~760px. On mobile, rail collapses to header icons only.

Feels like: a temple with a discreet altar in the corner — always present, never intrusive.

C · Full Sidebar (ChatGPT-style)

260px sidebar always visible with full thread titles, grouped by day. Main content narrows to ~700-760px. On mobile, sidebar collapses entirely to a hamburger drawer. Most conventional — most obviously a "chat app."

Feels like: a scholar's workbench — efficient, familiar, but loses some of the meditative singularity.
My recommendation

Option A — Overlay Drawer. It's the only option that preserves the current "single sacred column" feel you've already built carefully. The drawer pattern is also consistent across desktop and mobile, which simplifies CSS enormously. The trade-off is that history is one click away instead of zero — but for a reading-focused app where you're mostly in a thread, not switching between them, that's the right default. Option B is my second choice if you want the thread list to feel more present.
