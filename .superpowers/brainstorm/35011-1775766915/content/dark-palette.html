Pick the dark "candlelight" palette

The dark theme is the soul of the Sacred Interface. All three preserve saffron/gold as the sacred accent but differ in the base mood: warm temple, cinematic black, or midnight blue. Same verse tablet rendered in each — which feels right for evening reading?

A · Temple Ember ★

Deepest maroon base (#1F0808) with warm saffron ambient glows in the corners. The verse Devanagari has a subtle saffron text-shadow — it feels lit by an oil lamp. Most emotionally resonant. Warmest.

B · Midnight Gold

Near-black base (#0A0906) with real gold leaf accents — gold corner markers on the tablet, gold rule separator, gold reference text. Cinematic, dramatic, reverent. Feels like an illuminated manuscript. Most "premium."

C · Twilight Indigo

Deep blue-black base (#0E1018) with indigo ambient glow and saffron/gold only as accents. Feels like the sky just after sunset. Coolest of the three. More modern, less "temple."

My recommendation

A · Temple Ember. It's the only one that feels emotionally hot — the saffron glow in the corners and the text-shadow on Devanagari evoke real oil-lamp light. It's also the most honest extension of the existing warm palette you already love. Midnight Gold (B) is stunning but cold in the way luxury-brand websites are cold — beautiful, but less devotional. Twilight Indigo (C) is modern and pretty but drifts away from your maroon identity.
