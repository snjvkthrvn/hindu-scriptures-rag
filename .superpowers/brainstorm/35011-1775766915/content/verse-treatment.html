How should verse quotes look?

Four treatments for the same verse (BG 2.47) rendered in the light theme. The Sanskrit/Devanagari deserves to feel distinct from the surrounding prose — the question is how much distinction, and in what aesthetic register. Each treatment will have a matching dark-theme variant.

B · Inscribed Tablet ★

D · Editorial Pull-Quote

No card, no border. Just horizontal rules top and bottom, large centered Devanagari, small IAST + English, attribution at the bottom-right in gold. Feels like a serious literary magazine pull-quote. Most restrained.

My recommendation

B · Inscribed Tablet — distinctive enough to feel sacred without being costume-y. The centered layout, big Devanagari, and gold rule with diamond give each verse its own "stage" without being kitschy. Option D is my runner-up if you want something more restrained and editorial. Option C (palm leaf) is beautiful but high-risk: easy to tip into cliché. Option A is the safe non-choice.

Note: whichever you pick, the Devanagari will render much larger than today (14-18px vs current 11px) and each verse will have proper visual separation from the prose — that's a universal fix.
